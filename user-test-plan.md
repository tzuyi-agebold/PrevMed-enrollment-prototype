# Bold Health — Moderated User Test Plan
**Product:** End-to-end enrollment flow (Bold Health / Bold Care)
**Target participants:** Adults 65+, with chronic pain or a health condition
**Format:** Moderated 1:1 session — guide panel appears top-right of screen
**Session length:** ~45–60 minutes

---

## Before You Begin (Moderator Only)

Read this to the participant before starting:

> "Thank you for being here today. We're going to look at a website together. There are no right or wrong answers — we want to understand your honest reaction. If anything is confusing, that's very helpful for us to hear. You won't hurt anyone's feelings. Please think out loud as much as you can — just say whatever comes to mind as you go."

**Devices:** Use a phone or tablet to simulate the real experience.
**Screen guide panel:** Each task card appears in the top-right corner of the screen. Read it aloud to the participant if needed.

---

## Task 1 — Pick the Ad That Interests You Most

**On-screen card (what the participant sees):**
> Look at these 4 ads. Which one would you want to click on?
> **Go ahead and tap on it.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What made you choose that one over the others?"
- "What did you think this ad was offering?"
- "Was there an ad you definitely would NOT click? Why?"

**Single-choice question (ask verbally or show on screen):**
> What caught your attention first in the ad you chose?
> - The headline / main text
> - The image or video
> - Something personal that connected with me
> - I'm not sure

---

## Task 2 — Explore the Page

**On-screen card:**
> You clicked on the ad and landed here. Take your time to scroll through and read what you want. We're not in a rush.
> **When you're done, let us know.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "In your own words, what is this service about?"
- "Who do you think this is meant for?"
- "Was there anything that surprised you — good or bad?"
- "Was there anything that caught your eye right away?"
- "Is there anything here that was confusing or hard to understand?"
- "After reading this, would you want to keep going? Why or why not?"

**Multiple-choice question:**
> Which of these describes what you just saw? *(Select all that apply)*
> - I understand what this service does
> - I'm not sure if this is covered by my insurance
> - I trust this looks legitimate
> - I'm not sure who this is for
> - I want to learn more

---

## Task 3 — Check If You're Covered

**On-screen card:**
> Now let's see if you're covered. Find a button on this page that says something like "Check my coverage" and click it.

**Moderator observes:** How long does it take to find the button? Do they notice multiple CTAs?

**When modal appears:**
> Fill in any name and email address — made-up is fine.
> Then tap **"Check Coverage."**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What did you expect to happen when you clicked that button?"
- "Did anything feel surprising or unclear?"

---

## Task 4 — Enter Your State and Birthday

**On-screen card:**
> This page is asking for a little information.
> **Select any state. For date of birth, use: 01 / 15 / 1950.**
> Then tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Why do you think they're asking for this information?"
- "Did anything feel confusing or too personal here?"
- "Did you notice the 'Why are we asking this?' option? Did you want to tap on it?"

---

## Task 5 — See Your Coverage Results

**On-screen card:**
> Read through what's on this screen. Take your time.

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What is your first reaction when you see this page?"
- "What did you notice first?"
- "Does anything concern you or surprise you?"
- "What does the checkmark or insurance card make you think?"

**Single-choice question:**
> At this point in the process, how do you feel?
> - Confident — I want to keep going
> - Curious — I have some questions but I'm still interested
> - Hesitant — I'm not sure about this
> - I would stop here

---

## Task 6 — Select Your Health Focus Areas

**On-screen card:**
> This page is asking what health areas matter most to you.
> **Select everything that applies to you.**
> Then tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Was there anything missing from this list?"
- "Was it clear what you were being asked to do?"
- "Did any of the options feel surprising to see here?"

---

## Task 7 — Choose Your Top Priority

**On-screen card:**
> Now pick the **ONE** health area that matters most to you right now.
> Then tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Was it easy or hard to choose just one?"
- "Did the wording of the options make sense to you?"

---

## Task 8 — Tell the Doctor More

**On-screen card:**
> Read what this page is asking. You can write anything — there's no wrong answer.
> **Type whatever feels right, then tap Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "How did you feel about this question? Easy or hard to answer?"
- "Would you feel comfortable writing this for a real doctor's appointment?"

**Multiple-choice question:**
> How did this step make you feel? *(Select all that apply)*
> - It felt personal in a good way — like they want to understand me
> - I wasn't sure what to write
> - I'd rather not share this in writing
> - It gave me more confidence in the service
> - It felt like too much to ask

---

## Task 9 — Share Your Health Conditions

**On-screen card:**
> Select any health conditions that apply to you.
> If nothing applies, tap **"None of the above."**
> Then tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Was there anything missing from this list?"
- "Did sharing this feel comfortable or uncomfortable? Why?"
- "Did you understand why they're asking for this?"

---

## Task 10 — Meet Your Matched Doctor

**On-screen card:**
> Read through this page about the doctor you've been matched with.
> Take as much time as you need.

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What's your first impression of this doctor?"
- "What information made you feel more confident?"
- "Is there anything on this page that was confusing?"
- "Is there anything you'd want to know that you didn't see here?"
- "What would make you stop and not continue?"

**Multiple-choice question:**
> What matters most to you when reviewing a doctor? *(Select up to 3)*
> - Their photo and appearance
> - Their credentials and training
> - Their specialty or focus area
> - Patient reviews or ratings
> - How long they've been practicing
> - Whether they accept my insurance

**Single-choice question:**
> After reading this page, how likely are you to keep going?
> - Very likely — I'm ready
> - Somewhat likely — I have a few questions
> - Not sure yet
> - I would stop here

---

## Task 11 — Schedule Your Appointment

**On-screen card:**
> Go ahead and pick a time for your first visit.
> You can also choose between a **video call** and a **phone call.**
> Then tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Which did you choose — video or phone call? Why?"
- "Was it easy to find a time that works for you?"
- "Did having the choice between video and phone matter to you?"

**Single-choice question:**
> How do you feel about having your appointment this way?
> - I prefer this — I don't have to leave home
> - I'd rather see the doctor in person
> - I'm comfortable with either option
> - I'm not sure how this would work for me

---

## Task 12 — Review and Agree to the Forms

**On-screen card:**
> Read through what's on this page.
> Check the boxes and tap **Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "Did you read the forms or scroll past them? What made you decide?"
- "Did anything on this page feel unclear or concern you?"
- "Did you feel like you understood what you were agreeing to?"

---

## Task 13 — Add Your Phone Number

> *(This is the highest drop-off step — pay close attention to reactions)*

**On-screen card:**
> This page is asking for your phone number.
> You can use any number — made-up is fine.
> **Fill it in and tap Continue.**

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "How did you feel when you saw this page?"
- "What made you comfortable or uncomfortable sharing your number here?"
- "Did you read the blue box? Did it help you feel safer? Why or why not?"
- "What would stop you from sharing your real number at this step?"

**Single-choice question:**
> If this were real, would you enter your phone number here?
> - Yes — I feel comfortable with this
> - Probably yes, but I'd have a few concerns
> - I'd hesitate — I'm not sure why they need it
> - No — I would stop here

**Follow-up (if hesitant or no):**
- "What would need to change to make you feel comfortable?"

---

## Task 14 — See What Happens Next

**On-screen card:**
> Read through what's on this screen.
> Take your time — there's a lot here.

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What do you understand will happen next?"
- "What on this page was most helpful to you?"
- "Was there anything that confused you?"
- "Was there anything on this page you could remove and not miss?"
- "How do you feel after completing everything?"

**Single-choice question:**
> After seeing this confirmation page, how do you feel?
> - Relieved and ready — I know what to expect
> - Good, but I still have some questions
> - Unsure — I'm not clear on what happens next
> - Worried — I'm having second thoughts

---

## Task 15 — Read Your Text Message

**On-screen card:**
> This is the text message you'd receive after finishing.
> Go ahead and read it.

**Moderator prompts after:**
- "What do you think when you see this message?"
- "Would you answer a call from this number? Why or why not?"
- "What might stop you from picking up the call?"
- "Does the message feel trustworthy? What makes you say that?"

**Single-choice question:**
> If you received this text, what would you do?
> - Pick up the call — I'd be expecting it
> - Maybe pick up — depends on when they call
> - Let it go to voicemail first
> - Ignore it — I'd be unsure who was calling

---

## Wrap-Up Questions (Moderator Asks — No On-Screen Card)

Read these questions at the end of the session:

1. "Walk me back through the whole thing. Was there a moment where you almost stopped or wanted to give up?"
2. "Was there anything that was confusing at any point in the process?"
3. "Is there anything you expected to see that you never found?"
4. "In one sentence — what is this service?"
5. "Who do you think this service is meant for?"
6. "Would you sign up for this if it were real? What would make you more likely — or less likely?"

## Quick Reference: What We're Testing at Each Step

| Step | Key Learning |
|------|-------------|
| Ad selection | Which message/image resonates; what drives clicks |
| Landing page | Service comprehension; trust signals; value clarity |
| Check coverage CTA | CTA discoverability; expectation setting |
| State + DOB | Clarity of ask; privacy comfort |
| Coverage confirmed | Trust and surprise moments; insurance card comprehension |
| Focus areas | Label clarity; completeness of list |
| Primary focus | Decision difficulty; option framing |
| Free-form question | Comfort with open sharing; perceived purpose |
| Health conditions | Label clarity; privacy comfort |
| Doctor match | Trust drivers; information gaps; what causes hesitation |
| Schedule appointment | Channel preference (video vs. phone); ease of booking |
| Consent forms | Reading behavior; clarity; concern triggers |
| Phone number | **Highest drop-off** — privacy fears, trust signals, blue box effectiveness |
| Confirmation page | Next-step clarity; information density |
| SMS | Message trust; call-to-action clarity; pickup intent |
